Finding Work

How to get a job in Adelaide

Finding a job

Adelaide's job market can be tough for people new to the city, but there are a range of services available to help you learn new skills, find available positions and land your dream job! However, before looking for work, ensure your visa allows you to work in Australia!

Centrelink and JobActive

If you don't have a job, you can use Centrelink and the JobActive network to support yourself while you look for work. JobActive will help you improve your skills and your resume, and connect you with businesses who need workers. Centrelink offer a range of support payments for people experiencing unemployment - for most people, this will be Newstart.

Job Listings

Australia has a range of job listing websites, where you can search by job title, occupation or location to find jobs that are right for you. Adelaide also offers a number of 'gig economy' services, such as Uber, UberEats, Menulog and more.

Building skills

Feel like you need to learn new skills, or refine old skills, before you look for work? Adelaide has a range of services to help you build your resume and impress employers.

tafeSA is Adelaide's vocational training institution - offering a range of short and long courses, a tafe course can help you add new skills or help you start a new career AMES offer English language courses for migrants, as well as specialist skills trianing and employment preparation courses to help you find a job you love

Your rights as a worker

As a worker in Australia, you are protected by a range of laws that ensure you are paid fairly, kept safe in the workplace and treated with respect.

The Fair Work Ombudsman is a worker representative organisation that provides important information like award (minimum wage) rates, your rights as a worker, and help if you do experience a workplace incident. Australia has a range of workplace unions that will advocate for your rights as a worker. To learn more about unions in Australia, visit the Australian Unions page here

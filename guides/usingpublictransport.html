Using Public Transport

How to use our buses, trains and trams

Types of Transport

Trains

Adelaide has six train lines; heading from the Adelaide railway station (North Terrace) to Belair, Gawler, Tonsley, Grange, Seaford and Outer Harbor.

Trams

The tram line runs from the Adelaide Entertainment Centre (AEC) to Glenelg. The tram is free from the AEC to South Terrace and from AEC to Botanical Gardens (North Terrace). The tram is also free between Brighton Rd & Moseley Square, Glenelg.

Buses

To travel to many of Adelaideâ€™s metropolitan areas, buses are the best option. Buses come about every 15 minutes on routes close to the city and either every 1/2 hour or hour in the outer suburbs. Your MetroCard can be used on the bus, or you can purchase a ticket from the bus driver. A letter after the bus numbers (like F, X or T) indicate express buses that may not stop at all of the stops. Ask the driver if you are unsure. There is a free City Connector bus service around the city and North Adelaide.


Tickets

Paying your fare.

When you board public transport, all you need to do is touch your MetroCard against the yellow validator inside the door. At Adelaide Railway Station you will have to touch your card to enter and exit. Your fare will be automatically deducted from the value of your MetroCard.

You cannot purchase a MetroCard onboard, however a single trip and daytrip metro-tickets can be purchased on a bus from the driver. When paying your fare, please ensure you have change as bus drivers may not have change for larger notes.

Onboard your train or tram, you are able to purchase a single ticket from the ticket machine. You can also recharge your MetroCard onboard your train or tram using a ticket machine.

Please be aware that the ticket machines on board trains and trams do not accept notes or 5 cent pieces, however you can also pay using your debit/credit card.

Types of Tickets

There are two time periods of operation for public transport:

  • Peak travel times are before 9.01am, after 3.00pm Monday to Friday and all day on Saturday.
  • Interpeak travel times are 9.01am to 3.00pm Monday to Friday, all day Sunday and South Australian public holidays.

Where to buy a MetroCard

MetroCards can be purchased and recharged at Adelaide Metro InfoCentres as well as a range of agents, including supermarkets, newsagents and convenience stores. Recharging online is perhaps the easiest option, and you can also use that system to set up auto-recharge.


More Information

Timetables

The Adelaide Metro website is a great resource for train, bus and tram timetables. But, if you want real-time public transport information and updates on when your bus is due to arrive, make sure to download the metroMATE app.

Education

How to enrol in education, for kids and adults

Primary School

Primary Schools are for children aged 5-12. Adelaide has a wide range of primary schools in every neighbourhood, as well as Catholic and Private institutions. Education is mostly funded by the government, but some fees will apply, including paying for uniforms, extracurricular activities and excursions. Some schools will have a catchment area - if you live in this catchment area, your child will be given priority when enrolling for that school.

Secondary School

Secondary Schools (also known as High Schools) are for children aged 12-18, and attendance is compulsory for all students unless they are attending other training or are employed. Adelaide has a range of schools, public, private and Catholic. Students who finish their final year of schooling (Year 12) will attain their South Austalian Certificate of Education (SACE) and be elligible to attend universities around Australia.

Tertiary Education

Tertiary education in South Australia may be in the form of a university degree at one of our internationally recognised universities or a vocational course at tafeSA. Tertiary education is optional, but will help you build skills and experience for your career.

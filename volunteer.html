Volunteer

Want to help people move to our great city? Here are some ways you can help.

Multicultural Youth of South Australia

View their website here

MYSA run a range of services for young people and families from multicultural backgrounds, including youth mentoring, tutoring and homework support and more.

Australian Refugee Association

View their website here

The Australian Refugee Association have a wide range of volunteer roles, from Youth Mentoring to Administration Support to running Events. Volunteers must be aged 18 or over, are required to attend a volunteer induction session and provide contact details for two referees. All volunteers must undergo a National Criminal History/ Working with Children Check and attend relevant training specific to their role (provided by ARA).

Migrant and Refugee Settlement Services

View their website here

MARSS are always looking for volunteers to help them run their many support programs. All MARSS volunteers are also required to possess a Working with Vulnerable People (WWVP) Card, which is free for volunteers.

Refugee Council of Australia

View their website here

The Refugee Council of Australia is a small organisation. It relies on the support of dedicated volunteers, and offer a small number of volunteer positions, as well as providing a list of organisations all over Australia who need volunteers.

AMES Australia

View their website here

AMES Australia offer English language courses, practical assistance and vocational courses to newly arrived refugee families. They are always looking for new settlement volunteers, who provide support services such as helping families with every day tasks like shopping, using public transport and finding their way around the community, as well as navigating Centrelink, Medicare and healthcare services.
